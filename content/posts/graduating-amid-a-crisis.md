I won’t repeat the same spiel about these being unprecedented and extraordinary times as we have all head this sentiment countless times the past several months as COVID-19 has evolved into a global pandemic. As we enter May, we enter graduation season where I and countless other students around the country will be leaving our college careers behind us to enter the wider world. Of course, there have been no in person commencements or ceremonies because of the virus but I want to reflect on my own thoughts after listening to [this episode of 1A](https://the1a.org/segments/coming-of-age-in-the-age-of-coronavirus/)) this morning.

I’ve never been one for large ostentatious ceremonies so I was a little confused when they talked about the grief many college graduates are feeling because of the lack of ceremony, but as I thought about it I realized I did have some grief and that we don’t gather for the commencements. All of that is really very much just for show. I realized what I was feeling was this lack of closure to the past 4 or 5 years of my life.

The graduation ceremony, at least for me, is about putting the book end on a chapter of life, having a moment to celebrate that achievement, but most importantly to saying goodbye to our peers and professors who had become our friends, supporters, mentors and colleagues.

Now, as classes come to a close at slightly different times and communication sputters out, we’re left alone to look to the uncertain future. College is in some ways a safety net. While taking classes we get financial aid, a place to live, possible a part time job, and security in that we’ll be alright from one year to the next. We know what to expect. Even now, amid a global pandemic, this will blow over for most students. It will be hard for now, but they will go back to class and return to some level of normalcy.

There is no way to generalize the experiences of the thousands of college students dealing with this crisis, but at least for me, I still have my part time job with the university. I’m all but guaranteed to have this job for another 4 months, so all I can do is wait. What will the world be like in 4 months? Will the pandemic be over? Will I be able to find a job? Will the classes of 2020 ever get closure for their achievements? Will we get to meet up and bid one another farewell and our best wishes?

All any of us can do is wait and hope for a better future.
